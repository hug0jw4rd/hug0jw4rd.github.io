### Project Overview
As **Head of Mechanical Engineering**, I led the design and fabrication of an autonomous robot for the 2026 UniBots challenge: collecting and scoring spheres in a congested arena. The project culminated in a **3rd place overall finish** after a high-pressure, real-time strategic pivot on competition day.

### Engineering Challenges & Strategic Pivots

* **The Problem (Design Rigidity):** In the final hours before the heat, our complex delivery mechanism struggled with the arena's high congestion. Many teams failed by over-tuning failing systems.
* **The Solution (The "Strip-Down" Pivot):** I facilitated a team-wide decision to remove the scoring mechanism entirely. We pivoted to a "High-Speed Harvester" strategy, using a pre-planned magnetic collection array. This reduced weight, lowered the center of gravity, and maximized ball-acquisition points.

* **The Problem (CAD/CAM Workflow Friction):** Transitioning from SolidWorks to **Autodesk Fusion** while managing collaborative cloud assemblies led to version control conflicts and "blind" component editing.
* **The Solution (Design for Assembly):** I overhauled our file organization system, implementing a "Top-Down" design methodology. This taught me the vital importance of **Product Data Management (PDM)** and ensuring components are designed relative to their physical mating constraints.

* **The Problem (The "CAD Trap"):** We spent excessive time perfecting the digital twin, which delayed physical stress testing. This resulted in discovering tolerance issues only during the final assembly.
* **The Solution (Rapid Prototyping Mentality):** I implemented a "Fail Fast" protocol for future iterations, prioritizing low-fidelity physical tests over high-fidelity CAD renders to validate mechanical concepts early in the sprint.

### 👥 Leadership & Project Management
Managing a multidisciplinary team of 35 requires more than just technical skill; it requires **diplomatic technical oversight**. 
* **Timelines:** I bridged the gap between complacent timelines and hard deadlines by taking on "buffer" tasks to ensure system integration remained on track.
* **Adaptability:** Successfully managed the morale shift required to scrap months of mechanical work for a simplified, winning strategy on game day.
