### Project Overview
Developed over 48 hours, **Haptibelt** is a minimalist, sensory-integrated navigation system designed for high-altitude mountaineering. The goal was to remove the cognitive load and time wastage of traditional GPS navigation in extreme conditions, allowing climbers to maintain focus on survival and surroundings.

### Design Challenges & Engineering Solutions

* **The Problem (Cognitive Overload):** In extreme whiteouts, a climber’s ability to process visual data from a GPS screen is severely compromised by hypoxia and exhaustion.

* **Our Solution (Sensory Haptics):** We bypassed the visual cortex by shifting navigation to the **somatosensory system**. We engineered a chest-mounted haptic array that provides directional pulses directly to the user's core, turning navigation into a physical instinct.

* **Weight-to-Utility Optimization:** Every gram counts at 8,000 meters. We chose a low-profile, belt-integrated design over bulky wearable rigs, ensuring the hardware did not interfere with harnesses or oxygen systems.

* **Redundant Feedback Loops:** To account for high-vibration environments, we integrated secondary **peripheral visual cues** via LED indicators inside the goggles for 100% directional certainty.

* **Path-Back Logic:** We implemented a "bread-crumming" software that records the ascent path. In emergencies, the belt automatically reverses the path and the haptic and visual cues guide the user back to safety without ever having to takeout a GPS device.

### 👥 Team Contribution & Execution
Working in a multidisciplinary team, I focused on **problem identification** and **design ideation** along side **coding the visual feedback software**.
